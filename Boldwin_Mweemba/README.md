\# Zambia Macroeconomic Analysis — Impact of Debt Restructuring (2015–2025)



\*\*Tools:\*\* Power BI Desktop · Power Query · DAX  

\*\*Period:\*\* 2015–2025 | \*\*Country:\*\* Zambia



\## Overview



This project analyses Zambia's macro-fiscal transition across a decade of data (2015–2025),

with a focus on two central questions: Did debt restructuring restore investor confidence?

And how much of Zambia's economic recovery was copper-driven versus structurally anchored?



The analysis covers monetary policy, external trade, industrial composition, and sovereign

debt dynamics — built and visualised entirely in Power BI Desktop.



\## The analytical question



| Period | Phase |

|--------|-------|

| 2015–19 | Debt spiral — external debt tripled from $12.3B to $29.1B |

| Nov 2020 | COVID shock + Eurobond default (first African sovereign default of the pandemic era) |

| 2021–22 | Copper price supercycle + IMF ECF programme ($1.3B) approved |

| Nov 2023–24 | G20 Common Framework deal finalised; bilateral agreements implemented |



\## Key findings



1\. \*\*Copper front-loaded the recovery.\*\* The current account swung from -3.6% to +11.9%

&#x20;  of GDP between 2015 and 2021 — before the restructuring deal was signed.

2\. \*\*Debt restructuring locked in sustainability.\*\* PPG debt service fell from 12.5% to 3%

&#x20;  of exports post-deal. Growth of 5.2–5.4% persisted as copper prices cooled.

3\. \*\*Investor confidence confirmed.\*\* FDI net inflows hit a decade-high 9.32% of GDP in

&#x20;  2024, the same year bilateral restructuring agreements were implemented.

4\. \*\*The verdict: both were necessary.\*\* Copper created the fiscal breathing room; debt

&#x20;  restructuring converted it into a durable institutional framework.

\## Data sources



| Source | Coverage |

|--------|----------|

| World Bank WDI | GDP growth, debt, FDI, trade, inflation, exchange rate — 2015–2024 |

| IMF World Economic Outlook | GDP projections, Sub-Saharan Africa benchmarks |

| Bank of Zambia | Monetary Policy Rate, CPI inflation, ZMW/USD exchange rate |

| Ministry of Finance — Report on the Economy 2025 | 2025 data: copper production (0.89M t), inflation (7.50%), Policy Rate (14.25%), GDP composition |



> \*\*Note on 2025 data:\*\* Figures for copper production, inflation, and monetary policy draw

> specifically from the Ministry of Finance Report on the Economy 2025, which provides more

> recent data than the World Bank or IMF datasets at time of analysis.



\## Limitations



\- The 2026 forecast does not isolate climate-induced energy deficit impacts on industrial output

\- Global shifts in green metal demand (EV transition) are not fully captured in the model

\- Some 2022–2024 fiscal indicators are estimated where WDI data is not yet published
